# Technical Report: NFC Capabilities for P2P Payments on iOS

## Executive Summary
**Feasibility of Peer-to-Peer (P2P) NFC Payments: NO**

While iOS has recently opened up its NFC hardware to third-party developers, this access is highly restricted, regionally gated (primarily EEA), and specifically designed for **Card Emulation (HCE/SE)** intended for merchant terminals, not for device-to-device communication. True P2P NFC (ISO 18092) remains inaccessible to third-party apps on iOS.

---

## 1. Research Goals & Findings

### Does iOS allow device-to-device NFC communication?
**No.** True P2P mode (where two devices act as both initiator and target to exchange data) is not supported by Apple's `Core NFC` or the new `NFC & Secure Element` APIs.

### Core NFC Capabilities and Limitations
| Feature | iOS Support | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Reader/Writer Mode** | ✅ Yes | Can read/write NDEF tags, ISO 7816, 15693, FeliCa, MIFARE. |
| **Tag Emulation (HCE)** | ⚠️ Restricted | Only via `HCE Entitlement` (EEA only) or `NFC & SE API` (iOS 18.1+, requires commercial agreement). |
| **Peer-to-Peer (P2P)** | ❌ No | Standard ISO 18092 P2P is not exposed to developers. |
| **Background Scanning** | ✅ Partial | Supported for NDEF tags with Universal Links (iPhone XS+). User must tap notification. |

### Compatibility with React Native / Expo
The industry standard library `react-native-nfc-manager` works well for **Reader/Writer** tasks on iOS. However, it **cannot** trigger HCE or P2P on iOS because the underlying native framework does not support it for general use.

---

## 2. Technical Limitations & Security

### Why P2P fails on iOS
1. **API Abstraction**: `Core NFC` is a high-level framework that abstracts away the hardware. It does not provide the low-level access needed to implement the P2P protocol.
2. **Apple’s "Tap to Cash"**: Apple introduced a P2P payment feature in iOS 18 called "Tap to Cash," but this is a **closed system** within Apple Cash. No public API exists for third-party apps to hook into this hardware-level interaction.
3. **Entitlements**: Accessing NFC requires specific entitlements (`com.apple.developer.nfc.readersession.formats`). The new HCE/SE entitlements require a direct business relationship/approval from Apple and are not available for standard "hobbyist" or early-stage app development without significant legal/financial overhead.

## 4. Recommended Alternatives for P2P Payments

Since NFC-based P2P is not feasible for a generic third-party app on iOS, the following alternatives are recommended:

### 1. QR Codes (Recommended)
*   **Pros**: 100% cross-platform, no hardware restrictions, very low friction.
*   **Cons**: Requires camera access and decent lighting.
*   **Implementation**: One device displays a dynamic QR containing the payment URI; the other scans it.

### 2. Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE)
*   **Pros**: Proximity-based, works over several meters.
*   **Cons**: Higher latency for pairing/connection, permission-heavy (Bluetooth + Location).
*   **Implementation**: One device advertises as a peripheral; the other scans and connects to exchange payment data.

### 3. Deep Links & App Clips
*   **Pros**: Instant interaction via NFC tags or QR.
*   **Cons**: Requires a physical tag or another medium to trigger.
*   **Implementation**: An NFC tag (passive) triggers an App Clip that handles the payment immediately without the full app installed.

---

## 5. Conclusion & Product Direction
**Recommendation: Pivot to QR Codes or BLE.**

For the current scope of `Ding Payments`, building an NFC-based P2P flow on iOS would require:
1. Being based in the EEA (for HCE).
2. Applying for specific Apple entitlements (long approval process).
3. Paying fees and entering a commercial agreement (iOS 18.1+).

Even then, you would only be emulating a card for a merchant terminal, not talking to another phone. **QR Codes remain the gold standard for P2P mobile payments.**
